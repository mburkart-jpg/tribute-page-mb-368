Tribute to Hamilton Carhartt

Hamilton Carhartt

Hamilton Carhartt was an incredible individual who made a lasting impact on the world. Here is a brief timeline of his life achievements:

  • 1889 - Founded Hamilton Carhartt & Company in Detroit, Michigan, with just two sewing machines and five workers, focusing on durable workwear for laborers.
  • 1890 - Introduced the iconic "Carhartt Bib Overall," designed specifically for railroad workers, marking the start of high-quality workwear.
  • 1910s - Expanded the company with mills and sewing facilities in the United States and Canada, supporting local economies.
  • 1920s - Shifted production during World War I to support military efforts, including manufacturing uniforms.
  • 1930s -Successfully navigated the Great Depression by staying true to the company’s core values of quality and durability.
  • 1937 - Passed away, leaving behind a legacy of innovation in the workwear industry. The company continued to thrive under his family’s leadership.
  • Present Day - Carhartt remains a leading name in durable workwear, respected worldwide for its commitment to workers.

“I believe that when I make a sale, I make a permanent friend.” – Hamilton Carhartt
